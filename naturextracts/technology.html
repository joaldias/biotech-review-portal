State-of-the-Art Bioprocesses

Our eco-friendly, advanced processing technologies are engineered to extract and protect valuable compounds from raw biomass.

Supercritical CO₂ Extraction

Supercritical CO₂ extraction is a distinguishing and innovative technology for producing high-value compounds.

Operating as a high-pressure process at relatively low temperatures under a total absence of oxygen, it preserves temperature-sensitive active compounds from thermal degradation and oxidation.

CO₂ is food-grade and holds FDA GRAS (Generally Recognized As Safe) designation. Extracts obtained are sterile, highly pure, completely free from solvent residues, and require no preservatives.

Steam & Hydro Distillation

Used to extract 100% pure and natural essential oils and hydrolats from premium vegetable material.

In steam distillation, controlled steam removes volatile aromatic molecules at a rapid rate, preventing oil damage. Thermal degradation is avoided by strictly monitoring temperature and steam flow.

In traditional hydro distillation, water is used instead of vapor. In both methods, vapors are condensed to separate high-quality therapeutic compounds.

Lyophilization (Freeze Drying)

Lyophilization is a highly controlled water removal process used to preserve delicate bio-materials.

Products are first frozen and subsequently subjected to conditions of pressure very close to a vacuum, enabling the sublimation of water molecules directly from solid to gas.

This is the ideal choice for preserving temperature-sensitive compounds of biological origin—such as enzymes, blood plasma, and vaccines—dramatically extending shelf-life and ensuring transport convenience.

Bioreactor Fermentation

Fermentation is a natural biological mechanism carried out by targeted bacterial and yeast cultures to convert sugars into organic acids, alcohols, and high-value metabolites.

NatureXtracts applies state-of-the-art bioreactor control to expand fermentation technology for Pharmaceuticals, Cosmetics, and Bioenergy industries.

Key applications include target cell growth, production of fermentation derivatives such as exopolysaccharides and bioethanol, as well as controlled enzymatic hydrolysis.
