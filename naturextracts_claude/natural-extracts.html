Supercritical CO₂ Extraction

Supercritical CO₂ extraction is a distinguishing and innovative technology for producing high-value compounds. It is a high-pressure process working at relatively low temperatures — producing high-quality extracts since the integrity of temperature-sensitive compounds is maintained — and under total absence of oxygen, which fully preserves the valuable natural extracts.

CO₂ is food-grade and considered GRAS (Generally Recognized As Safe), an American Food and Drug Administration designation, meaning that a chemical or substance added to food is considered safe. Extracts produced through Supercritical CO₂ extraction are sterile, highly pure, completely free from solvents, and need no preservation.

Steam & Hydro Distillation

Steam distillation is commonly used to extract essential oils from vegetable material. In this extraction method, steam removes the aromatic molecules from the biological material at a faster rate, preventing oil damage. Degradation of the essential oil is also avoided by controlling the temperature and the amount of steam applied to the matrix.

Hydro distillation is one of the most traditional methods of extraction. Instead of steam, water is used to extract essential oils, but in both methods the vapors of the volatile components are carried by the steam to a condenser.

Algae & Plant Extracts

Algae are currently being researched for their potential and are already considered a sustainable source of valuable natural functional ingredients — such as pigments, proteins, antioxidants, fatty acids, and vitamins — with additional health benefits for humans and animals.

Some of the versatile compounds extracted from plants and seaweeds are used as functional ingredients in the human and animal food, nutraceutical, pharmaceutical, and cosmetic industries.
