The Science

How the Ingredients Support Immunity

A closer look at how each nutrient in the NX Immunity Essentials formulation contributes to immune system support.

Choline

  • Choline is one of the latest discovered essential nutrients and can be seen as vitamin-like. It acts like a vitamin in the body, but it is not part of a hormone or a co-enzyme and is therefore not considered a 100% vitamin.
  • Recognised as absolutely essential by the FDA (the Food and Drug Administration) and the EFSA, as both concluded that approximately 90% of the population is deficient in choline.
  • For the immune system, choline is required for synthesis of the main cell membrane phospholipid, phosphatidylcholine.
  • Studies (1), (2) and (3) underline the importance of the nutrient choline in the function of a specific type of immune cell called a macrophage, particularly for cell membranes and in cell signalling. When these cells sense a foreign invader, choline metabolism is activated. On the flip side, limiting how much choline is available can make these cells more pro-inflammatory.

Vitamin D

  • Vitamin D plays a regulatory role in the functioning of the immune system.
  • Intake of vitamin D contributes to the normal function of the immune system and a healthy inflammatory response.

Vitamin C

  • Vitamin C contributes to immune defence by supporting various cellular functions of both the innate and adaptive immune system.

Natural Tocopherol (Vitamin E)

  • Vitamin E is a chain-breaking antioxidant that protects membranes from free-radical damage.
  • Evidence suggests a significant impact of vitamin E on the modulation of immune functions.
  • Results from animal and human studies indicate that vitamin E deficiency impairs both humoral and cell-mediated immune functions.
  • Supplementation of vitamin E is associated with increased resistance against several pathogens.

Zinc

  • Zinc deficiency is associated with a decline in immune function. Zinc deficiency results in a loss of B-cell and T-cell precursors within the bone marrow.
  • Zinc supplementation in humans has shown benefit in immune responses to bacterial and viral infections.
  • Zinc bisglycinate is an organic mineral amino acid chelate that has been scientifically proven to be more easily absorbed. Part of the reason for this higher bioavailability and tolerability is that chelated minerals are absorbed intact and break apart in the intestinal cell for transport.

Selenium

  • Dietary selenium, mainly through its incorporation into selenoproteins, plays an important role in inflammation and immunity.
  • Adequate levels of selenium are important for initiating immunity, but they are also involved in regulating excessive immune responses and chronic inflammation.
